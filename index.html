SAIGON
A CROSS-CULTURE CUISINE

“Tell me what you eat, and I shall tell you who you are.” - Brillat-Savarine, a French gastronome
Vietnamese cuisine can be described in three words: Diverse, Sophisticated, and just purely Delicious. With favourable environment conditions, plentiful natural resources, and important geographical positions, Vietnam attracted foreign merchants (and well, colonizers), which consequently drived cuisines from all over the world to gather in Vietnam. Since then, Vietnamese have adopted the quintessential of those cultures and transformed them into what we have nowadays: A Cross-cultural cuisine. Representing the diversity and dynamics of Vietnamese cuisine is Saigon, where each and every dish has a fascinating story to tell, and versions to try out!

Xiu Mai


Xíu Mại, which has its origin from Chinese “shumai”. In Cantonese, "shumai" means "cook and sell," indicating that this delicious dish is often enjoyed at eateries rather than homemade (Filek et al., 2017). However, different from the other version, Xíu Mại in Saigon does not have the dough wrapping, making it a savory dish to serve with Vietnamese baguette or hot rice.

Bo Kho


Vietnamese Beef Stew is a variant of Indian curry, touched by French culinary influence during colonial times (Nguyen & Hoang, 2019). Vietnamese people have a different preference when it comes to curries compared to Indians. Indian curries are known for their thick, aromatic, rich, creamy, and spicy qualities. However, Vietnamese curries incorporate lemongrass, sweet potato, and taro, catering to the Vietnamese sweet tooth. As a result, the Beef Stew enjoyed in Vietnam offers a hybridized taste that is distinct from those curries consumed in India. The ingredients of the dish vary widely based on preferences. It can be served with Vietnamese baguette, rice, noodles and herbs.

Banh Xeo


Bánh Xèo, despite its unknown origin, is generally believed to be inspired by Cham cuisine, or the fusion of French culture (Filek-Gibson, 2020). The name “xèo'' originates from the sound when the layer of rice batter touches the hot pan and fries to a crispy texture. In each region, the pancakes vary in size and ingredient composition, resulting in a wide range of distinctive and delicious versions of "bánh xèo”. There are different versions of the dish, ranging from the small, yet thick round pieces of pancakes in Central Vietnam, to the bigger, yet thinner, with more variety of fillings as in the South. In any way, the dish is served with a savory fish sauce.

Hu Tiu


Or Hủ Tiếu, is a Southern dish inspired by Teochew cuisine when the people migrated to Vietnam (Ngọc, 2016). It can be served as a noodle soup with a rich broth, or noodle salad without it. There are different types of noodles and ingredients served with the dish, which creates multiple versions of it, such as: Phnom Penh Hu Tiu, Shacha Hu Tiu, My Tho Hu Tiu, Sa Dec Hu Tiu,...

Banh Mi


Bánh Mì is a short baguette that was first presented to Vietnam during the French colony (Linh, 2020). Despite being a luxury item in the past, the dish is now the most loved street food not only by Vietnamese, but also by international peers. Different types of fillings are used for the dish, which creates multiple versions for it. There are Shredded Pork Banh Mi, Xiu Mai Banh Mi, Chicken Banh Mi, Fried-egg Banh Mi, … Besides, there is also a sweet version of the dish called Ice Cream Banh Mi.

Pho


Pho is the most famous Vietnamese dish that has captured the love of all food lovers. It is believed to originate from the intersection of various cultures. The dish has a huge collection of variants, inspired by the distinction of regional culinary factors, and different cooking techniques (Pham, 2015). Besides Nothern Pho and Southern Pho as a well-known comparison, there are Wine-sauced Pho, Sauteed Pho, Rolled Pho, Mixed Pho, Dipping Pho, Sour Pho,…
Saigon cusine is a snapshot of how colorful Vietnamese cuisine can be. It is transformative throughout time and absorbing values of cultures, as well as history. Besides being “finger-licking good”, Vietnamese dishes are also healthy and nutritious. If you come to Saigon, do not miss out on seven out of thousands of dishes we recommended!
